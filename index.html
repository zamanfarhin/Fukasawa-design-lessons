Naoto Fukasawa

Born in 1956 in Kōfu, Yamanashi Prefecture, Fukasawa studied product design at Tama Art University, graduating in 1980. He spent his early career at Seiko Epson before moving to San Francisco in 1989 to join ID Two, the design firm that became IDEO. Seven years there, mostly serving Silicon Valley clients, shaped how he thinks about behavior and objects.

He returned to Japan in 1996 to found and head IDEO's Tokyo office, then went independent in 2003, establishing Naoto Fukasawa Design and launching his own product brand, ±0. He has sat on MUJI's advisory board since 2002, and is art director at Maruni. His work is held in the permanent collections of MoMA, the V&A, and the Philadelphia Museum of Art, which staged a major retrospective of his practice in 2024.

He has designed for Herman Miller, Alessi, B&B Italia, Driade, Magis, Artemide, HAY, and dozens more — moving fluidly between electronics, furniture, interiors, and architecture without ever losing the same quiet hand.

His philosophy

Without Thought

Fukasawa's central belief: design should emerge from people's unconscious behavior. An object shouldn't announce its importance on first sight — it should reveal itself only through use. He has run annual workshops under this name since 1999.

Design dissolving in behavior

Design isn't self-expression. It's observation — watching how people actually move through their day, and finding the solution already embedded in that behavior, so object and person become linked without a manual required.

Super Normal

Co-developed with Jasper Morrison in 2006. An exhibition and a stance: everyday objects stripped of excess branding, prioritizing seamless integration into daily life over standing out.

Design should blend into the background of everyday life. Naoto Fukasawa

An original wellness object, designed in Fukasawa's language. Every branch below traces back to the same root idea.

Without Thought
The amulet band
Form
Oval bangle, Cartier Love-inspired but stripped of hardware, screws, branding. An archetype made quietly better, not invented.
Material
Rose gold or silver steel, embedded jade dots. Jade already carries meaning — protection, continuity — no applied branding needed.
Closure
Made-to-order sizing. Hinge and invisible latch both sit skin-facing — mechanism tucked from view, never presented outward.
Three states
Dormant, jade and steel, nothing showing. Raised, a scrollable read on the wrist-lift gesture. Emergency, the one moment it breaks its own silence.
System split
The band only senses. The paired app thinks — data, contacts, alerts live there. The object itself stays pure.
Gesture
Raise to view — the same motion already used to check a watch. No new behavior to learn, nothing to interrupt.
  • FormOval bangle, Cartier Love-inspired but stripped of visible hardware, screws, or branding. An archetype made quietly better, not made to look invented.
  • MaterialRose gold or silver steel body, embedded jade dots. Jade carries its own cultural meaning — protection, health, continuity — so the object needs no applied branding to feel significant.
  • ClosureMade-to-order sizing. Hinge on the skin-facing side, invisible latch on the opposite skin-facing point. The mechanism is honest, but placed where it never interrupts the calm of the outer face.
  • Three statesDormant by default — jade and steel, nothing showing. Raised — the same gesture already used to check a watch surfaces a scrollable set of quiet widgets. Emergency — the one moment the object breaks its own silence, because safety outranks the ask-first rule everywhere else.
  • System splitThe band senses. The paired phone app thinks — holding the data, the contacts, the notifications. The physical object stays pure.
  • GestureRaise to view, the same motion already used to check a watch. No new behavior to learn.
